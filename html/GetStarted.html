Feeling overwhelmed? I'll break it down for you!


Struggling on a name?

I suggest starting with a name you like and being open to changing it later as their personality develops. Using name generators is a great starting point! Here is my personal favorite that contains names based on race and heritage.


What is a class?

Think of a class as your character’s profession and personality. This determines their abilities and skills which directly leads to how they are played. Reference the bottom of the page to get a feel on each classes goals.


Choosing a race

Each race has distinct characteristics, abilities, and lore within the D&D universe. This can determine your backstory or how you play the character after finding further information on their D&D heritage. Please reference the bottom of the page to get a brief summary of each race.


What are character backgrounds?

Every character has a backstory before joining a party of adventurers. What drove them from their home? What made them who they are, and what fuels their goals? Each background grants you different combinations of abilities, proficiencies, and skills. This part is easiest to reference on the official D&D Beyond website or another page that contains a good summary for each.


Character ability scores
-This is where it gets technical!-

Your stats determine how you play your character and shape their personality in conversation and combat. In other words, it’s how you interact with the world as you play. There are six stats used to create your character: Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma. The higher the number for one of your stats, the more proficient you are in that skill, so make sure to use them according to how you want to play your character! What does each of the six stats represent? Reference the bottom of the page for a simplified explanation.


Creating a Backstory-

Similar to your character's background, you'll dig deeper into what brought them to where they are now, but without receiving any extra bonuses. This is for your reference and your characters lore. Perhaps you have a secret about your past that you don’t want to tell others. Maybe your character has a specific personality. Is family important to them? Do they have a family? Remember! Most importantly, this is for you! Please be as creative as you’d like and have fun.

Further Information

While new classes emerge, these are the most common and widely used Base Rule classes.


Barbarian | “A Fierce Warrior or Primal Rage” |
Warriors that excel in melee by harnessing their primal forces of rage on their enemies.

Bard | “An Inspiring Performer of Music, Dance, and Magic” |
Expert performers that inspire others, soothe with their honeyed words, dishearten foes, and and create illusions in combat.

Cleric | “A Miraculous Priest of Divine Power” |
Priests who channel magic from the Outer Planes and use it to help others and destroy those who dare defy them.

Druid | “A Nature Priest of Primal Power” |
They call on the forces of nature to harness the magic of healing, animal transformation, and elemental destruction.

Fighter | “A Master of All Arms and Armor” |
Contain an unparalleled skill with weapons and armor, they are well acquainted with death, both causing it and defying it.

Monk | “A Martial Arts of Supernatural Focus” |
Focusing their internal reservoirs of power to create extraordinary, even supernatural, effects in combat.

Paladin | “A Devout Warrior of Sacred Oaths” |
Warriors of the Divine who thrive on the front lines of the cosmic struggle, united by their oaths against the forces of annihilation.

Ranger | “A Wandering Warrior Imbued with Primal Magic” |
Contain a deadly focus and harness primal powers to protect the world from the ravages of monsters and tyrants.

Rogue | “A Dexterous Expert in Stealth and Subterfuge” |
Stealthy warriors who always have a trick up their sleeve. Have a knack for finding solutions to any problem and prioritizing subtle strikes over brute strength.

Sorcerer | “A Dazzling Mage Filled With Innate Magic” |
Their power forms in their very being. Born with the innate power for magic, they harness it into a raw and rolling power in combat.

Warlock | “An Occultist Empowered by Otherworldly Pacts”|
Harnessing the power of a Divine and channeling it in their search for knowledge, their Divine deal is stamped into their very being.

Wizard | “A Scholarly Magic-User of Arcane Power” |
Contains the runes and spells of explosive fires, arcing lightning, deception, and spectacular transformations from studying the scrolls.

D&D is a vast world, there are other races that aren't listed here, but these are the widely recognized Base Rule races.


Dragonborn | An average height of 6 foot, 3 inches |
The ancestors of Dragonborn hatched from the eggs of chromatic and metallic dragons. They stand tall in a humanoid form with scales, but lack wings or a tail at times.

Dwarf | An average height of 4 foot, 1 inch |
Raised from the earth in the elder days by a deity of the forge. They are resilient and tough folk who work as blacksmiths and miners, specializing in creations.

Elf | An average height of 5 foot, 5 inches |
The curiosity of elves led many to explore other planes of existence. With extended life spans, they have diversified into Wood Elves, High Elves, and Dark Elves (Drows), with these being the most common.

Gnome | An average height of 3 feet, 4 inches |
Magical folk created by gods of invention, illusions, and life underground. They have high energy and enthusiasm for living as it shines through every inch of their small body.

Goliath | An average height of 7 foot, 1 inch |
Distant Descendants of giants and seek heights above those reached by their ancestors. Their towering heights and incredible strength and endurance lends to their competitive nature, facing anything as a challenge.

Halfling | An average height of 3 feet |
Possess a brave and adventurous spirit that leads them on journeys of discovery. Of small stature they are affable and cheerful people. They cherish the bonds of family and friendship as well as the comforts of hearth and home.

Human | An average height of 5 foot, 7 inches |
Found throughout all realms, humans are as varied as they are numerous. They are resourceful and ambitions which lends them to be respected, feared, or commended across worlds.

Orc | An average height of 6 foot, 1 inch |
Of larger stature, they have lower tusks and immense strength to lead Orc tribes. Some venture into the world in order to prove their worth with their gifts to help them wander Great Plains, vast caverns, and churning seas.

Tiefling | An average height of 5 foot, 6 inches |
Originated from the Lower Planes, like Avernus, whether their ancestors came from their or themselves were born there. They are often treated with stares and whispers, suffer violence and insult on the street due to their vibrant skin and eyes, horns, and the occasional tail.

A brief summary on each Ability Score’s purpose.

Strength - How physically strong your character is. (Athletics)

Dexterity - How skilled your character is in movement and body control. (Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand, and Stealth)

Constitution - Your character's overall health and physical capability.

Intelligence - Your characters ability to recall information they have read or learned for a long time. (Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, and Religion)

Wisdom - The practical knowledge that your character puts into use rather than only reading about them. (Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Perception, and Survival)

Charisma The force your character’s personality has causing people to like you. (Intimidation, Performance, Persuasion, and Deception)

Not enough detail?

Dnd Beyond is a free website that is a great starting place for new and experienced players alike. Any base rules and texts on how to play are free, however there are paid options for specific upgrades and expansions to the D&D universe The 5e player handbook is a great start! It outlines details about classes, races, backgrounds, and the inter-workings of combat and your character's statistics.

Perhaps you want to create a more permanent character. Dnd Beyond also contains a great character creator for free once you create an account. The account will allow you to keep up to 5 characters before having to pay for a subscription for more slots. This website is easy to use, especially since each step is clear. Alongside the player handbook, your Dnd character will take shape in no time.

Once you have created your Dnd character, it's fun to mess around with how they will appear. A website that is a great use for visualizing your ideas is heroforge where you can create mini figures. Heroforge has dozens of options for races and features to bring your character off the page. While the website encourages you to purchase the mini you've made, it's not necessary. It is a free tool unless you decide to purchase your creation.
