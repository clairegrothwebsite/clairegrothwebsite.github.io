History from 59 D.C. to Present P.Q.

The 7 realms are ruled by Queen Olympia De Rosa, a crown that has been passed down through generations since 59 D.C. starting with Haemus De Rosa. Before the De Rosa's rule of Savand, there were 3 countries that span over current Savand: Avaria, Stiyp, and Thirund. These countries were peaceful until a pillar appeared where the 3 countries boundaries met. This pillar was created of bone with a skull topped upon its cap. History has since named it the Pillar of Athanasia, after the ancient goddess of destruction and judgement who was said to be beheaded in a war of Gods. This pillar was said to influence mortals and cause the persons hatred and selfish desires to be their only thoughts. The influence of the pillar got into the minds of the leaders, causing their minds to only focus on their desire for power: including the want for this pillar to be their own. The leaders fought at that point in front of the pillar, thus starting the battle of the powers. Avaria gained the pillar after defeating the others and retreated behind their walls. The Siege of Avaria began as the other rulers obsessed over the pillar's secrets and how Avaria might use this against them. Drafts of civilians were called to battle as they stormed the boarders of Avaria. Behind the walls, the pillar's power has spread. People brainwashed to do the will of the pillar who wanted nothing but destruction. From within the bone, the power of the goddess emerged allowing for Avaria to wield the power of destruction and judgement. Outside the walls, units of men, women, and soldiers vaporized as they were judged on their life. This was outlined as the darkest point in Savand's history as friends turned against each other and people vanished at the thought of the pillar. This initial siege lasted for 3 years until a civilian put it to a stop. History outlines an Avarian that withstood the power of the pillar and snuck into the castle where it was held. There he destroyed the power of Athanasia and broke the controlling power that held the lands. War seemed to stop instantly, people wandered confused about where they were and how they got there. This Avarian was named Haemus De Rosa, who then became the leader of Avaria. The leaders of Avaria, Stiyp, and Thirund soon each took a piece of the Pillar of Athanasia and hid it among their territories. It took Haemus De Rosa 59 years to rebuild what the pillar and greed destroyed, and only then did the countries rebuild themselves as Savand. They entered the current age of peace and trade amongst new civilizations and open boarders for all races and people.

Ortar

The only Federation inside of Savand where each city’s land governs themselves separately under the central monarchy of Savand. This realm spans from the Golden Mountains of the South-West to the North-Eastern Star Sea. In between lies flat plains and flower patches that span between the mysterious forests of the realm. Along the coast lies the fishing and trading cities that spread their goods across the realm. These federations used to live peacefully before Bastion Keep had enough. Now, the Federation of Bastion Keep threatens to tip the balance and take Otrar by force to make it theirs. The cities remain on edge, but refuse to get involved directly to stop their reach. Inside these cities are a range of extravagant builds depending on the city. Castles are made of beautiful stone work inside the Ehoral Kingdom. In Bystead buildings are constructed out of cobblestones and logs, creating a quaint atmosphere in the town. This realm is very diverse in races and magical abilities, however the communities tend to stick together inside of cities and forests. This can lead to hostility and uncertainty when explorers go into removed communities looking for answers.

Taradlands

Lead by their Lord Blaine Atwood, Taradlands prides themselves as the military of the people. This selfless country has recently dedicated themselves to preventing the war from spreading outside of Shonia. The cities are highly developed nations with magical colleges and military bases. These are signs of the peaceful stature of Taradlands who no longer needs to focus on survival and instead values knowledge and personal joy. The realm contains the steep cliffs of Engar Summit with towns and cities clinging to its edge. Plains span the rest of Taradlands with forests spanning the southern edge. 90% of all civilians center themselves in cities that range from a few hundred to thousands, leading to all kinds of races and classes to be in one city. Tieflings, Orcs, Humans, Dwarves, and Half-Elves protect and contribute to the rich culture of these cities. The forests remain wild and undeveloped and untouched from mortal hands.

Kingdom of Thiir

Lead by Lady Eleanore Smithe, Thiir is the largest and most extensive realm of Savand. Known as the trade central, it holds the most diverse and extensive cities with all races and professions. This realm is best known for their well developed cities as all of them are created from stone and contain thousands of citizens. Outside of the cities, mansions scatter the plains along with villages that still house upwards of a thousand citizens, showing Thiir's economic security and prosperity. Along the Eastern edge of Thiir sits the Shadhaara desert. People dare not to enter as legend says that’s where the old powers began grappling for power over the Pillar of Athanasia . While Thiir is the economic and trade central that connects all of Savand, they tend to deal with their problems and no one else’s. Their military stands stagnant, patrolling the boarders and cities to ensure order and safety. Inside of all cities are immigrants and people who call themselves citizens of Thiir. High elves, halflings, humans, half elves and other races mingle and live within the borders.

Floura

Floura is a wild country that is lead by the Avington family. Thick forests of pine and oak cover 90% of the realm: Nanmore Grove, Styip Hallow, and Lashwe Wood are the most wellknown. Dryads, Satyrs, Centaurs, Gnomes, Orcs, and Wood Elves roam these enchanted woods. While there is peace in these lands, there is also crime and a lack of organization between villages. Scattered across the forests are quaint villages and towns of wood where citizens make their living through small businesses. These citizens are work driven and often struggle for security as Floura’s trade is limited to what the forest can provide. There are a handful of well developed cities that disperse any trade and offer jobs to those who need it, however they lack a military and they often contain dangerous crime groups. Not all the forests have been explored, but stories have circulated about the dangers of the unknown deep woods

The Holy Findlaire Empire

Lead by a priest of Helm, Horace Alden, this realm is dedicated to the deities of the Divine Realm. This peaceful country provides a sanctuary for worshipers who wish to spread their religion across the people and realms. The capitol city Helmfire is dedicated to the worship of Helm, the god of protection. This city sits ontop Pinnacle Peaks , while beautiful valleys souround the isolated mountain range. Mountain passes cut through the Celest Range and connects this realm to others. Scattered across the land are shrines dedicated to different gods. Villages and cities are often established near a chosen deity's shrine for worship and in hopes the deity will bless their town. Welcoming to all races, primarily Elves, Genasi, Dwarves, and Humans call this place home. The large cities have developed themselves from the original shrines of holy cites and made elaborate and beautiful cities to reflect the gods ideals and personification. Inside of cities, citizen mingle and religions merge. While not everyone is accepting, there is little to no violent outbreaks within Findlaire.

Sirdaz

Voted by the people, Sir Roman Farraday watches over Sirdaz from the only city in the land, Silvergal. Inside its walls lies the trading port of the realm. Trading stands, merchants, and adventurers all flood into this port to reach the shores of Savand. The main purpose of this city is to provide payment to citizens for the traded goods that are dispersed across Savand and beyond. Outside of the city lies villages surrounded by fields of different agriculture. Sirdaz is the main provider of agricultural needs across the realms and citizens spend their lives working the fields. Humans, Gnomes, Goliaths, and Halflings call these places home. Citizens all work together, ensuring that their fields provide a hearty harvest and are protected from any raiders. These fertile lands cover 90% of the realm while the Enchanted Tips cover the North-East with sporadic forests in between. This country is peaceful between farmers, neighbors, and citizens, but raiders often scavenge fields for their goods and to take the profit for themselves. Fires and destruction has began to spread as these raiders gain a name for themselves.

Shonia

Overthrown by Drow leader Haelra Tuin, this realm has been ridden with war as the Drow search for a long lost artifact that was dug from the Underdark beneath Shonia. They are surrounded by Bahamut Peak, The Celest Range, and the Towering Tips on all sides except the west where a river flows into the realm. 100 meters under the surface, the Underdark lies where its like stepping into a new world. Uncovered by Dwarves digging for precious gems and materials for their elaborate cities, the artifact was dragged from its home in Drow territory. Before being taken over, this realm was home to the Dragonborns and Dwarves. These races worked together to create elaborate stonework palaces, cities, and architecture only to have it lay in rubble from the destruction of the Drow. These cities have been taken over with crude remakes of sacred buildings created by the enslaved Dragonborn civilians. The war has left the population scattered and ensalved until the Drow take back what was rightfully theirs. They will go to any length to take back what they desire most, including taking over all of Savand.
